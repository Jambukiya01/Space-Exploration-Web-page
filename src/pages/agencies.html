02 Space Agencies

National Aeronautics and Space Administration - (United States)

NASA

NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is the United States' space agency. Founded in 1958, NASA conducts space exploration, scientific research, and aeronautics advancements. Its missions include crewed spaceflights, robotic explorations of other planets, and studying Earth's climate and space phenomena. NASA's achievements have revolutionized our understanding of the universe and inspired global scientific progress.

Indian Space Research Organisation - (india)

ISRO

ISRO, the Indian Space Research Organisation, is India's space agency established in 1969. It conducts space missions, satellite launches, and space research. ISRO achieved significant milestones like the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) and Chandrayaan missions to the Moon. It aims to advance space technology and benefit humanity through various applications, including communication, Earth observation, and scientific research. ISRO's cost-effective approach and successful missions have earned international recognition and contributed to India's scientific and technological growth.

European Space Agency - (European Union)

ESA

ESA, the European Space Agency, is an intergovernmental organization established in 1975. Comprising 22 member states, it conducts space exploration, satellite launches, and scientific research. ESA's missions include Rosetta, which landed a probe on a comet, and the Hubble Space Telescope collaboration. ESA aims to advance European space capabilities, foster international cooperation, and contribute to scientific advancements for the benefit of humanity.

China National Space Administration - (china)

CNSA

CNSA, the China National Space Administration, is China's national space agency established in 1993. It conducts space exploration, satellite launches, and scientific research. CNSA's missions include the Chang'e lunar program, which landed rovers on the Moon, and the Tianwen-1 mission to Mars. CNSA aims to expand China's space capabilities, foster international collaboration, and explore the mysteries of the cosmos. Its accomplishments have contributed to China's growing prominence in the field of space exploration.

Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency - (japan)

JAXA

JAXA, the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency, is Japan's national aerospace and space agency established in 2003. It conducts space exploration, satellite launches, and research in aeronautics and space technology. JAXA's missions include Hayabusa, which collected samples from asteroids, and the Kibo module on the International Space Station. JAXA aims to contribute to scientific advancements, enhance Japan's technological capabilities, and promote international cooperation in space exploration. Its achievements have solidified Japan's position as a key player in the global space community.
